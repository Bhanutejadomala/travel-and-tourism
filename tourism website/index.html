Temples And Places

Shri Ram Temple Ayodhya,UP

Ayodhya is a city situated on the banks of holy river Saryu. In the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, It is the headquarter of Ayodhya District.Ayodhya is an ancient city of India, is the birthplace of Bhagwan Shri Ram and setting of the great epic Ramayana.

Mahakaleshwar Temple Ujjain,MP

One of the seven sacred Hindu cities, Ujjain is located on the banks of river Kshipra and is land of the magnificent Kumbh Mela. Iconic religious sites and a web of lively lanes are a vibrant display of Ujjains art and heritage.

kedarnath Temple Kedarnath,Uttarakand

According to Hindu mythology, the Kedarnath temple was built by the Pandavas, the heroes of the Hindu epic, the Mahabharata, as a way to atone for their sins. Lord Shiva, who was being pursued by the Pandavas, took the form of a bull and disappeared into the ground at Kedarnath.

Dwarkadhish-Temple Dwaraka,Gujarat

Dedicated to Lord Krishna, this temple is originally believed to have been built by his grandson, Vajranabha over his residence, Harigriha in ancient Dwarka. It is one of the famous Char Dhams in Hindu mythology and is built on the banks of River Gomti.

Tirupati Temple Tirupati,AP

The temple is devoted to Lord Sri Venkateswara, a Vishnu incarnation who is thought to have come to this site to rescue humanity from the trials and difficulties of the Kali Yuga. As a result, the place was given the name Kaliyuga Vaikuntham, and the regional god is known as Kaliyuga Prathyaksha Daivam.

Puri Jagannath Temple Puri,Odisha

The traditional story concerning the origins of the Jagannath temple is that the original image of Jagannath, a form of Vishnu, at the end of the Dvapara Yuga, was manifested near a banyan tree, near the shore in the form of an Indranila Mani, or the Blue Jewel.
